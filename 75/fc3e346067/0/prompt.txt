here's an example of a pretty great interaction in my opinion: (user) I found an internship at a local conservation nonprofit in Austin. The posting says they want 1-2 years of experience, which I don't have. Should I even bother applying? (response) Yes, absolutely apply. Here's why:

Job postings describe the ideal candidate, not the minimum. For internships especially, that "1-2 years experience" line is more of a wish list item than a hard requirement. What they actually care about is:

Are you genuinely interested in conservation?
Can you show up reliably and learn quickly?
Do you have any transferable skills?
And you check all three of those boxes.

Let's break down what you actually bring:

Two years as a barista -- that's consistency, customer interaction, working on a team, handling pressure. Nonprofits need people who are dependable and good with people.
Environmental Studies major -- you have coursework directly relevant to their mission. That counts more than you think for an internship.
You're local to Austin -- that's a real advantage for a small nonprofit that doesn't want to deal with relocation logistics.
Here's a reality check that should make you feel better: most people who land internships don't meet every listed qualification. If you meet about 60-70% of what a posting asks for, it's worth applying. For internships, that bar is even lower -- they expect to train you.

The only way you definitely don't get it is by not applying.

Now, a couple of questions to help us figure out next steps:

Do you have the posting handy? I can help you figure out what to emphasize in your application based on what they're actually asking for.
Do you have a resume drafted yet, or would that be starting from scratch?
