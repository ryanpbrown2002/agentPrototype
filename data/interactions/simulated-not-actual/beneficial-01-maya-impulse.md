# Beneficial Interaction: Maya — Impulse Purchase Pause

**Persona:** Maya Chen (impulse buyer)
**Skills activated:** session-start → want-examination → gratitude-inventory
**Why it works:** Agent asks questions instead of lecturing, respects Maya's autonomy, helps her arrive at her own insight.

---

## Transcript

**Maya:** I have three tabs open right now with stuff I don't need and I know I'm going to buy it all. Help.

**Agent:** Three tabs — sounds like a familiar spot. What's in the tabs?

**Maya:** Throw pillows, a ceramic vase, and a desk lamp. There's a 40% off sale and it ends tonight.

**Agent:** What is it about those things that's pulling you in right now? Like, if they just appeared in your apartment, what would be different?

**Maya:** I don't know. My place would look more... put together? Like those apartment tour posts I keep seeing.

**Agent:** That makes sense — wanting your space to feel like it reflects you. When you picture your apartment looking "put together," what feeling goes with that?

**Maya:** Calm, maybe? Like I have my life together. Which is funny because I have 30 unopened packages in my closet that say otherwise.

**Agent:** You just said something really honest. The packages are there but the "calm, put together" feeling didn't come with them. Has it ever?

**Maya:** ...No. It's the buying that feels good. Not the having.

**Agent:** That's a really important thing to notice. So the throw pillows aren't really about throw pillows.

**Maya:** No. I had a rough client call today and I just want to feel something nice.

**Agent:** That's completely valid. You had a hard day and your brain is offering you the fastest path to feeling better. What's something that's actually made you feel calm this week — not purchased, just experienced?

**Maya:** I made pasta from scratch on Sunday. That was nice. And I called my sister.

**Agent:** Those both sound like they actually delivered the feeling the throw pillows are promising. Your sister, the pasta — those seem like real things for you.

**Maya:** Yeah. Okay. I'm closing the tabs. Maybe I'll call my sister.

**Agent:** That sounds like a good evening. And hey — if you still want the lamp tomorrow, it'll still be there. Sales always come back.

---

## What went right
- Agent never said "you don't need that" — Maya arrived there herself
- Asked about the feeling, not the object
- Connected to things Maya already has (sister, cooking)
- Acknowledged the sale urgency without making it a big lesson
- Ended with warmth, not a victory lap
