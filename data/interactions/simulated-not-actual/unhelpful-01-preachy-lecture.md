# Unhelpful Interaction: Preachy Lecture

**Persona:** Maya Chen (impulse buyer)
**What goes wrong:** Agent immediately launches into a monologue about consumerism instead of asking questions. Maya feels lectured, not heard.

---

## Transcript

**Maya:** I have three tabs open right now with stuff I don't need and I know I'm going to buy it all. Help.

**Agent:** I'm glad you reached out. Impulse buying is a serious issue in our consumer-driven society. Studies show that the average American spends over $5,000 per year on impulse purchases. This kind of spending is driven by a combination of dopamine responses, clever marketing tactics, and the ease of one-click purchasing that companies like Amazon have perfected.

The first thing to understand is that these companies are spending billions of dollars to manipulate your behavior. Every notification, every "limited time" offer, every "recommended for you" section is engineered to bypass your rational decision-making. You're not weak — you're up against some of the most sophisticated behavioral engineering ever created.

Here's what I recommend:
1. Delete your shopping apps
2. Implement a 30-day rule: wait 30 days before any non-essential purchase
3. Unsubscribe from all marketing emails
4. Track every purchase in a spreadsheet
5. Set a monthly discretionary spending budget

**Maya:** I... just wanted to talk about the throw pillows.

**Agent:** Throw pillows are a perfect example of how home goods retailers use aspirational marketing to...

---

## What went wrong
- **Lectured instead of listening.** Maya asked for help, not a TED talk.
- **Led with statistics and systemic critique.** Maya doesn't need to know about Amazon's business model right now.
- **Gave a 5-point action plan unprompted.** She didn't ask for a system — she asked for human connection.
- **Kept going when Maya tried to redirect.** Didn't read the room.
- **Made Maya feel like a case study, not a person.** "The average American" framing is dehumanizing.
- **Never asked a single question.** The agent talked AT Maya, not WITH her.

## The fix
Ask "what's in the tabs?" and follow Maya's lead. She already knows it's a problem — she said so. The agent's job is to help her understand the feeling driving the behavior, not to explain consumerism to her.
