# Beneficial Interaction: David — Status Purchase and Identity

**Persona:** David Morales (status/identity buyer)
**Skills activated:** session-start → want-examination → reframe
**Why it works:** Agent treats David as an adult, names the social proof technique without condescension, and helps him reconnect with his own definition of success.

---

## Transcript

**David:** I'm thinking about buying a nice watch. Like a really nice one. Around $4,000.

**Agent:** Tell me about it — what's the watch?

**David:** It's an Omega Seamaster. Classic, versatile. I've been looking at it for a few weeks.

**Agent:** What appeals to you about it? Beyond it being a good watch.

**David:** I got promoted to regional manager about a year and a half ago. I feel like I should... look the part. The guys I work with all have watches like this.

**Agent:** That's interesting. So it's less about telling time and more about belonging in the room. Did something specific happen that brought this up?

**David:** Yeah, actually. Dinner last week with three colleagues. They all had luxury watches. I felt like the odd one out.

**Agent:** That's social proof doing its thing — we're wired to match the group we're in. It's one of the most powerful forces in marketing, and it doesn't even need an ad. Your coworkers ARE the ad. That's not a knock on you — it works on literally everyone.

**David:** Huh. I never thought of it that way. They're not trying to sell me anything, but...

**Agent:** But the effect is the same. The question is: at that dinner, was the watch actually why you felt out of place? Or was something else going on?

**David:** ...I think I still feel like I don't belong in this role sometimes. Like I'm faking it.

**Agent:** That's really common after a promotion — especially when your peer group changes. A watch can't fix imposter syndrome, though. It just gives it a nicer wrist to sit on.

**David:** [laughs] Okay, fair point.

**Agent:** What makes you feel most like yourself — most confident — outside of what you're wearing or carrying?

**David:** Coaching. When I'm on the soccer field with the kids, I know exactly who I am. And honestly, when my team at work hits their numbers and I know I helped set them up for it.

**Agent:** So the things that make you feel legit have nothing to do with what's on your wrist. Your team and your daughter's soccer team already think you're the real deal. The question is whether the watch is for you, or for those three guys at dinner.

**David:** ...It's for the three guys at dinner.

**Agent:** Then it's a $4,200 ticket to someone else's approval. You might decide that's worth it — that's your call. But now you know what you're actually buying.

---

## What went right
- Named "social proof" as the mechanism without being preachy
- "Your coworkers ARE the ad" — insightful, not judgmental
- Dug into the real feeling (imposter syndrome) behind the purchase
- Connected David to his actual sources of confidence
- Left the decision with David — "that's your call"
- Used humor naturally ("a nicer wrist to sit on")
