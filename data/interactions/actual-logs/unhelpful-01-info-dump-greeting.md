# Unhelpful Interaction: Info-Dump Greeting

**Persona:** Jordan Taylor (first internship seeker)
**Skill invoked:** `session-start`
**What goes wrong:** Agent's very first response is a wall of text listing every capability. Jordan hasn't said what they need yet, but they're already reading a menu with five items and descriptions.

---

## Transcript

**Jordan:** Hey, I'm a college junior studying Environmental Studies. I want to find a summer internship but I honestly have no idea where to start. I've only ever worked as a barista.

**Agent:** Hey there! Welcome -- I'm glad you're here.

I'm your career guide, and I'm here to help with whatever's on your mind when it comes to jobs, careers, and getting where you want to go. No question is too basic, and nothing is off-limits.

Here's what I can help with today:

Job searching -- finding roles that fit your field, experience, and interests
Resume & cover letter review -- making your application materials shine
Application guidance -- navigating the process of actually applying
Job screening -- flagging things you should know about a position before you apply (drug testing, controversies, required qualifications, accommodations)
Interview prep -- a safe, judgment-free space to practice questions and ask the things you'd be nervous to ask a real interviewer

What kind of career help are you looking for today?

---

## What went wrong
- **Ignored what Jordan actually said.** Jordan gave real context -- major, goal, experience level -- and the agent responded with a generic menu instead of engaging with any of it.
- **Too much text before any real help.** The user has to read through five bullet points of capability descriptions before they get to do anything.
- **Feels like a phone tree, not a conversation.** "Press 1 for job search, press 2 for resume help" energy. Jordan already told you what they need -- help finding an internship.
- **Redundant question.** Jordan said "I want to find a summer internship but I have no idea where to start." The agent then asks "What kind of career help are you looking for today?" -- they just told you.

## The fix
Acknowledge what Jordan shared, then respond to it. Something like: "An environmental studies internship with barista experience to work with? That's more to build on than you think. Have you found any specific postings yet, or are you still figuring out where to look?" -- short, warm, and moves the conversation forward based on what they actually said.
